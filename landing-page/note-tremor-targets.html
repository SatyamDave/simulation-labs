Tremor and target size: a Monte-Carlo study of click miss-rates

Satyam Dave, Udaya Vijay Anand

Technical note · Jul 2026 · Simulation Labs

Abstract. We study how our tremor actuation model interacts with target size, in an offline Monte-Carlo simulation (20,000 trials per cell, seed 0, a 1280×800 viewport). This exercises jitter_coords, the same function that adds motor noise in production — not the live vision model. With a tremor of σ=14px, a click misses a 44px target (the WCAG 2.2 AA minimum) 20.5% of the time and misses a 24px target 60.9% of the time; holding the miss-rate under 5% at σ=14 requires targets of at least 62px. Mean radial error at σ=14 is 17.59px. We read these as rank-order signals about where risk sits, not as predictions of real-world miss-rates.

1 Setup

Our tremor channel is a single function, jitter_coords: given a target coordinate it adds independent Gaussian noise N(0, σ px) to the x and y axes, then rounds and clamps the result to the viewport. In this study the model is assumed to aim at the exact centre of a square target; jitter then perturbs the landing point. A trial counts as a miss if the landed point falls outside the target box on either axis.

This is a simulation of the actuation noise model in isolation. No screenshots, no network, and no Holo inference are involved — we are characterising the motor-noise stage of the pipeline, not the model's behavior on a real page. We ran 20,000 trials per (σ, target-size) cell, shared across target sizes within a σ, with np.random.default_rng(0). σ=0 was verified as an exact identity (0% miss). The σ values shown correspond to real personas: grandma-72 uses σ=4, impatient-mobile σ=6, and the tremor persona σ=14; others use 0.

Two thresholds are worth naming. At σ=14, the 44px AA-minimum target is missed one click in five (20.5%), and the 24px target is missed more than half the time (60.9%). Sweeping target size at σ=14, the smallest target that keeps the miss-rate under 5% is 62px — roughly 1.4× the AA minimum.

3 Implication

Target size is a design lever with a measurable effect on this model. Within the simulation, enlarging a control from the 44px minimum toward ~62px moves the σ=14 miss-rate from about one-in-five to under one-in-twenty. That is the kind of change this study is meant to surface: a rank-order, where-the-risk-is signal that says which controls are most exposed to motor noise and which direction a fix should go.

It is not a real-world prediction. The percentages here are properties of a Gaussian noise model aimed at an idealised target centre, not forecasts of how often any person will miss a button. Use them to compare designs and prioritise, not to quote an expected failure rate.

4 Limitations

This study simulates the actuation-noise stage only. It assumes the model aims perfectly at the target centre and that error is isotropic Gaussian, so it excludes everything the live model contributes: localisation error, the effect of perturbed perception on where the model aims, dwell, re-tries, and page dynamics. Real interaction error is not simply Gaussian. Results are single-seed (seed 0) and specific to a 1280×800 viewport and square targets. Treat the numbers as characterising our tremor model, not human motor behavior.
