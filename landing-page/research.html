Research

These are honest technical notes on how Simulation Labs works and what we have measured so far. They are work in progress, not peer-reviewed, and every number is tagged in-text with how it was produced (offline Monte-Carlo, synthetic labeled traces, a small live probe, or measured in rehearsal).

Jul 2026 · Technical note

Mechanical fidelity: degrading perception and action instead of prompting personas

S. Dave

Prompted personas match real users only about 11.86% of the time (arXiv 2503.20749); we instead mechanically degrade a screenshot-in / action-out model's channels, which lets us keep coordinates exact.

Jul 2026 · Technical note

Tremor and target size: a Monte-Carlo study of click miss-rates

S. Dave

In an offline Monte-Carlo of our tremor actuation (20,000 trials per cell), a 14px jitter misses a 44px target 20.5% of the time and needs targets of at least 62px to hold the miss-rate under 5%.

Jul 2026 · Technical note

Perturbation invariants and pipeline cost

S. Dave

Offline: our perturbations preserve image dimensions and are byte-deterministic (100%, 8/8), the perception pipeline costs a median 61.4 ms/frame, and the stuck-detector scored perfectly on 225 synthetic labeled histories.
