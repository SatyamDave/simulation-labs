the say–do gap

Surveys can't tell you where they quit.

Every "synthetic user" tool asks AI personas what they think of your page. The answers are shallow and people-pleasing — prompted personas match real user behavior only 11.86% of the time (arXiv 2503.20749). Meanwhile real money leaks from your highest-intent flows: an average 70.19% of shoppers who reach checkout abandon before they pay, yet checkout design changes alone can lift conversion by up to 35% (Baymard Institute, 2025). You can't fix a drop-off you can't see.

Sources: arXiv 2503.20749 (prompted-persona fidelity); Baymard Institute checkout UX research, 2025 (70.19% average cart abandonment; up to 35% conversion lift from checkout design changes).

behavioral personas

Real behavior, not roleplay.

Each persona attempts your task with its perception and actions mechanically shaped to match how real people behave — so failure is measured, not imagined.

  • Rushed Races ahead, skips instructions, bounces the moment it stalls. races · bounces
  • Distracted Wanders off task, backtracks, loses the thread mid-flow. wanders
  • Misclick-prone Jittery taps that land just off the target. jitter
  • First-time visitor Reads everything literally, assumes nothing about the UI. hesitates
  • Mobile (thumb precision) Small screen, fat-finger taps near tight targets. small viewport
  • Slow connection Waits on loads, times out when the page lags. latency

Baselines — neutral controls

  • Fluent power user No constraints; the flow works, this proves it. control
  • AI agent No constraints, machine-driven: is your flow usable by an agent at all? control
