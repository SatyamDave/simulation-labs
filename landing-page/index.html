Simulation Labs is a research lab studying how real users behave, by running mechanically degraded computer-use agents against real flows to find where people abandon.

Every synthetic-user tool today captures what people say; we simulate what they do. We take a pre-trained computer-use model and degrade its perception and action channels — blur, coordinate jitter, tight step and time budgets — so it attempts your real task and either finishes or rage-quits at a specific pixel. We don't claim psychological fidelity; we claim mechanical fidelity, not psychological fidelity — the perceptual and motor constraints are real even if the personality is synthetic.

Mechanical fidelity, not psychological fidelity

S. Dave

Prompted LLM personas match real users only about 11.86% of the time, so we degrade the perception and action channel of a computer-use model rather than prompt a personality.

Jul 2026 · Technical note

Tremor and target size

S. Dave

In an offline Monte-Carlo simulation, click jitter at σ=14px misses a 44px target 20.5% of the time, and targets need to be at least 62px to hold the miss-rate under 5%.

Jul 2026 · Technical note

Perturbation invariants

S. Dave

Every perception transform preserves image dimensions (8/8) and is byte-identical across runs, so degrading what the model sees never remaps the coordinates it clicks.
